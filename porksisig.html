Sisig Recipe

Sisig is a Filipino dish made from pork parts, usually pig’s head and liver, seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers. It is a popular dish in the Philippines and is often served as an appetizer or a main dish, typically sizzling hot on a cast iron plate.

The dish has a perfect balance of savory, tangy, and spicy flavors, making it a favorite among locals and tourists alike. Sisig is often enjoyed with rice and sometimes topped with a raw egg and chicharrón (crispy pork skin).

Ingredients

  • Pork belly or pork face (boiled and grilled, then chopped)
  • Pork liver (boiled and chopped)
  • Onions (finely chopped)
  • Calamansi or lime juice
  • Chili peppers (chopped)
  • Soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper
  • Mayonnaise (optional)
  • Egg (optional)
  • Chicharrón (optional, for topping)

Step by Step Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pork

    Boil the pork belly or pork face and liver until tender. Grill the pork belly or pork face until crispy, then chop into small pieces. Chop the pork liver.

  2. Mix the Ingredients

    In a bowl, combine the chopped pork, liver, onions, calamansi or lime juice, chili peppers, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Mix well.

  3. Cook the Sisig

    Heat a bit of oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the pork mixture and cook, stirring occasionally, until heated through and slightly crispy.

  4. Serve
