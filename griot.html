Griot Recipe

Griot is a popular Haitian dish made from marinated and fried pork shoulder. It is typically served with rice, beans, and pikliz (spicy pickled vegetables). The marinating process infuses the pork with a blend of citrus and spices, resulting in a flavorful and tender meat.

After marinating, the pork is fried until crispy on the outside while remaining juicy on the inside. Griot is often enjoyed at celebrations and gatherings, showcasing the rich culinary traditions of Haiti.

Ingredients

  • Pork shoulder (cut into chunks)
  • Orange juice
  • Lime juice
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Scotch bonnet pepper
  • Thyme
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Step by Step Instructions

  1. Marinade

    Combine orange juice, lime juice, minced garlic, Scotch bonnet pepper, thyme, salt, and black pepper in a bowl. Add the pork and coat it well. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  2. Prepare to Fry

    Remove the pork from the marinade and let it come to room temperature. Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium-high heat.

  3. Fry the Pork

    Fry the pork pieces in batches until they are golden brown and crispy, about 5-7 minutes per batch.

  4. Rest and Serve

    Let the fried pork rest on paper towels to drain excess oil. Serve with rice, beans, and pikliz.
